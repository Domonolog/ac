    2. in plain words

      If the characters on your website have a low color contrast ratio, people with visual impairments or color-blindness will find it very difficult to navigate your site.

      For example, someone who is color-blind will view the green and red apples in the top image as simply yellow apples, as demonstrated in the image below.

      Therefore, your website’s color contrast ratio must be in compliance with the WCAG’s guidelines.

    3. WHOM DOES THIS AFFECT?

      People with visual impairments or color-blindness.

    4. HOW TO SOLVE IT:

      Option 1 - Do it yourself:

      Make sure all text on your page meets the minimum color contrast ratios specified by WCAG:

      • 3:1 for text that is 18 pt, or 14 pt and bold
      • 4.5:1 for all other text

      Option 2 - Use automated solutions:

      In order to ensure that your website is in compliance with WCAG guidelines and is accessible for people with visual impairments, you will need to either fix accessibility issues yourself or employ an automated widget. Solving the issues manually requires re-writing code and working with your programmers and designers. This is a relatively costly and time-consuming task. Alternatively, you can add a widget to your website that enables users to alter the visual content of the site to meet their needs. This is a win-win for the website owner: with almost no added cost, your website will be compliant with the law and disabled users will be able to navigate safely and effectively.

      Users who suffer from seizures need to be able to freeze all moving parts of the website to prevent seizures.

    3. WHOM DOES THIS AFFECT?

      People with certain neurological issues, specifically people who suffer from seizures

    4. HOW TO SOLVE IT:

      So instead of changing the entire website to be free of GIFs and other triggering content, the widget gives individual users the freedom to customize the website to their needs, without changing the code of the entire site.

    2. in plain words

      Links must have a unique name or short description attached to them in the code to enable screen readers to read out loud. Otherwise the screen reader will simply read out loud “link,” and the user won't have a contextual understanding of where this link leads.

    3. WHOM DOES THIS AFFECT?

      People who use screen readers, including the blind and visually impaired

    4. HOW TO SOLVE IT:

      Option 1 - Do it yourself:

      Similar to buttons, links primarily get their accessible name from their text content. Avoid filler words like "Here" or "Read more;" instead, put the most meaningful text into the link itself.

      Option 2 - Use automated solutions:

      Instead of fixing each link by yourself, you can use automated solutions to make changes quickly and efficiently. An automated solution will:

      • Scan your website and check if links have names or descriptions attached to them.
      • The solution will use AI technology to understand what the link is.
      • The solution will add an accessible name automatically if one of your links is missing it.

      When you use an automated solution, you won’t ever have to worry again about accessibility. The solution will automatically scan your website every 24 hours and fix any new uploaded content .

    2. in plain words

      Your website must allow users to use the browser zoom function so that the visually impaired are able to read the text of your website. The browser zoom function will be disabled if you did one of the following:

      1. Made the “User-scalable” parameter =”no” in the <meta name=viewport”>

      2. Put the [maximum-scale] attribute at less than 5.

    3. WHOM DOES THIS AFFECT?

      People with visual impairments

    4. HOW TO SOLVE IT:

      Option 1 - Do it yourself:

      Remove the user-scalable="no" parameter from the viewport meta tag and make sure the maximum-scale parameter is set to 5 or greater.

      • 3:1 for text that is 18 pt, or 14 pt and bold
      • 4.5:1 for all other text

      Option 2 - Use automated solutions:

      You can simply harness the power of automated solutions that enable users to zoom in or out, change fonts, scale content and much more in order to have a better experience in your website, according to their needs.

      Never worry about the zoom function again!
